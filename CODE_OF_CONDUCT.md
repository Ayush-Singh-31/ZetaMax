# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

ZetaMax is built in public, and everyone should be able to participate without harassment or intimidation. Contributors, maintainers, reviewers, and community members are expected to make this a thoughtful, constructive, and welcoming project.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, specific, and patient.
- Critique ideas and code, not people.
- Assume good intent while remaining open to correction.
- Make room for different backgrounds, experience levels, communication styles, and perspectives.
- Give credit for ideas and work.
- Protect private information and respect reasonable boundaries.
- Accept a maintainer’s decision gracefully, even when you disagree.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, stalking, or sustained disruption.
- Discriminatory language or conduct.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or attention.
- Insults, personal attacks, trolling, or deliberate humiliation.
- Publishing another person’s private information without permission.
- Pressuring someone to disclose personal information.
- Retaliation against someone who reports a concern.
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional community.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in project repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, and other spaces represented as part of the ZetaMax community. It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project elsewhere.

## Reporting

Do not debate conduct concerns in a public issue. Report them privately to the maintainer using a private contact method available on the maintainer’s [GitHub profile](https://github.com/Ayush-Singh-31).

Include the relevant links, dates, a factual description, and any supporting context. Reports will be handled as privately as practical. Anyone involved in the report will recuse themselves from deciding its outcome.

For software vulnerabilities, use the separate process in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, or other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct. Depending on impact and pattern, a response may include:

1. a private clarification or correction;
2. a formal warning;
3. a temporary restriction from project interaction;
4. permanent removal from project spaces.

Decisions will consider context, severity, intent, harm, and prior behavior. Maintainers are responsible for applying these standards fairly and for explaining moderation decisions when doing so is safe and appropriate.
